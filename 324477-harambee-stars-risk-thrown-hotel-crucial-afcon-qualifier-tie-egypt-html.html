Harambee Stars risk being thrown out of hotel ahead of crucial AFCON qualifier tie against Egypt

 Posted on February 10, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  475 words  |  Almeda Bohannan

- Harambee Stars AFCON qualifier campaign is off to an awful start in Egypt

- The players are reportedly on the verge of being kicked out of their hotel in Cairo over unpaid bills

- Kenya faces Egypt on Thursday, November 14

The Harambee Stars quest to qualify for the African Cup of Nations is facing embarrassing challenges as players are reportedly on the verge of eviction from their hotels in Egypt.

Kenya is set to face Egypt on Thursday, November 14 but the players are having to deal with sub plots which may well take them out of their game.

Nation reports that several players are being evicted from their hotelrooms for delayed payments – a predicament confirmed by Football Kenya Federation (FKF) secretary general Barry Otieno.

"Since Friday, Ministry officials have been promising they will settle the payment, regrettably this has not happened,” Otieno said as quoted by Nation.

A contingent of 33 Kenyans are currently residing at the said hotel in Cairo including skipper Victor Wanyama and striker Michael Olunga.

At least KSh 4 million is needed to sort the arreas whicn include hotel bills in what seems to be a renewed arm wrestle between the Football Kenya Federation and the Sports docket.

FKF boss Nick Mwendwa commented on Harambee Stars woes by simply saying the whole situation was a sorry one.

"Such level of unseriosness ! I can only ask Kenyans to help me call out this disregard for our athletes and players," he tweeted.

Mwendwa was recently involved in a spat with Sports PS Kirimi Kaberia over funding of national teams, with both parties trading blame.

On one hand, the FKF boss blamed the Sports ministry for frustrating national teams by blocking funding whilst Kaberia maintained no funds would be released untill the FKF accounted for KSh 244 million which was allegedly received to cater for the Africa Cup of Nations.

As it stands, the fate of Kenya's performance in their first match en route to qualifying for the AFCON remains in the balance, although odds have now tilted amazingly to Egypt's side.

Notably Harambee Stars had to endure a 16-hour flight to Cairo whilst they would have accommodated in a far more comfortable five hour direct flight
