Ministry of Roads Announces 8 Venues for Public Participation to Increase Road Maintenance Levy

 Posted on February 20, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  529 words  |  Hettie Henneman
  • The Ministry of Roads and Transport and the Kenya Roads Board (KRB) are inviting public participation in reviewing the Road Maintenance Levy Fund (RMLF)
  • The proposed amendments aim to raise the Road Maintenance Levy on petrol and diesel from KSh 18 to KSh 25 per litre
  • Citizens are encouraged to submit their written comments and memoranda by July 5, 2024

Elijah Ntongai, a journalist at TUKO.co.ke, has more than three years of financial, business, and technology research expertise, providing insights into Kenyan and global trends.

Nairobi– The Ministry of Roads and Transport, in conjunction with the Kenya Roads Board (KRB), has issued a public call for comments and participation in the review of the Road Maintenance Levy Fund (RMLF) Order, 2016.

The Cabinet Secretary for Roads and Transport Kipchumba Murkomen and the National Treasury back the proposal to amend the levy, which aims to increase the Road Maintenance Levy on petrol and diesel.

What is roads maintenance levy?

The Road Maintenance Levy Fund, established by the Roads Maintenance Levy Act of 1993, finances the maintenance of roads in Kenya.

This fund is managed by the Kenya Roads Board, which was created under the Kenya Roads Board Act of 1999.

Public participation venues

The Ministry has scheduled a series of public forums across various regions on Monday, July 8 2024, from 9a.m. to 5p.m.

These forums are designed to engage citizens and stakeholders, ensuring their input is considered in the proposed amendments in line with Article 10 of the Constitution of Kenya, which mandates public participation in governance.

The public forums will be held at the following venues:

  • Nairobi: 8th Floor, Boardroom, Transcom House (Ministry Headquarters)
  • Central: KRB Regional Office, Off Ruringu Road, Nyeri
  • North Rift: KURA Regional Office, Pioneer-Cherunya-Lungas Road, Eldoret
  • South Rift: KRB Regional Office, 1st Floor, Public Works Building, Nakuru-Sigor Road, Nakuru
  • Upper Eastern: KRB Regional Office, Off A10 Isiolo-Wajir Road, Isiolo
  • Lower Eastern: KeRRA Regional Office, Off Makatano Chumvi Machakos, opposite Machakos School, Machakos
  • Nyanza: KRB Regional Office, Mezzanine Floor, Lake Basin Mall, Kisumu-Kakamega Road, Kisumu
  • North Eastern: KRB Regional Office, Off A3-Boys Town Secondary School Road, Garissa
  • Coast: KeNHA Regional Office, 2nd Floor, Ministry of Public Works Building, Off Shimanzi Road, Mombasa

The ministry also encouraged citizens to send their submissions of written memoranda and comments, which can be emailed to magumbi@krb.go.ke or hand-delivered to the Director General, Kenya Roads Board, Kenya Re Towers, 6th Floor, Upperhill, Nairobi.

The submissions must be received by Friday, July 5, 2024, at 5p.m.

Murkomen supports increasing levy

The increase in the levy was proposed in the Finance Bill 2024. CS Murkomen, appearing before the Departmental Committee on Finance and National Planning, said that RMLF collections are not sufficient to maintain the growing network of roads.

He supported the proposal to review the levy from KSh 18 charged on every litre of petrol and diesel to KSh 25.

However, during the second reading of the Finance Bill 2024, MPs noted that the RMLF could not be amended through the Finance Bill.
