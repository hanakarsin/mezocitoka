Kenyan MPs debate regulations to tame farting during flights

 Posted on May 27, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  578 words  |  Enda Jantzen

- The proposed regulations aim to improve comfort of passengers who travel aboard local and international flights

- Rangwe MP Achieng Gogo proposed that passengers be served with medication that deals with stomach discomfort to reduce flatulence

- The proposal also wants to empower states to deal with unruly passengers who commit other offences aboard flights

Members of the National Assembly are debating the adoption of a motion that envisions to deal with unruly passengers who cause discomfort to their colleagues during flights.

The motion dubbed Approval of Ratification of Protocol to Amend the Convention of Offences and Certain Acts Committed on Board Aircraft (Montreal Protocol, 2014) is undergoing public participation in the House's Transport Committee.

Among behaviors the proposal seeks to deal with are abuse of other passengers and crew, and damage of equipment.

Lawmakers supporting the bill claimed such incidents have been rife and pose a security threat to both local and international flights.

"The purpose of the Protocol is to empower States to deal with unruly passengers, while preserving prosecutorial discretion. This will enhance civil aviation safety and security by extending jurisdiction to the State of landing and State of Operator.Clarifying certain behaviors which should be considered, at a minimum, as an offense and encourages States to take appropriate criminal or other legal proceedings. Recognising that airlines have a right to and may seek compensation from unruly passengers for costs incurred as a result of their unruly behavior where this involves diversions to disembark an unruly passenger," read part of the motion.

Farting, according to Rangwe MP Lilian Achieng Gogo, was among habits that caused a lot discomfort to passengers especially during long hauls.

Achieng added the menace had also crawled inside local fights pointing out Kisumu-Nairobi and Kisumu-Mombasa routes as most affected.

To tame careless flatulence, the lawmaker proposed that passengers be given special medication that would eliminate or reduce the urge to fart.

“We need to pass this bill so that whoever is taking a flight will be taking Eno or be compelled to take sodium bicarbonate which is used as antacids in neutralising excess stomach acid. Of late some people here have been making the environment worse while travelling to Kisumu and Mombasa,” Achieng said claiming that she has also been a victim of such experience.

Achieng further informed the committee chaired by David Pkosing (Pokot South MP) that safety and comfort could further be enhanced by deploying paramedics on board.

Such medics, the MP said, will go a long way in providing first aid but also pointed out the importance of equipping crew with knowledge on the best food to be served to passengers.

“We need special training on aircraft crew so that they provide medicines like bicarbonate of soda to passengers after meals and drinks have been served. We should also have paramedics who are trained in basic first aid included in the international and local flights,” Gogo said.
