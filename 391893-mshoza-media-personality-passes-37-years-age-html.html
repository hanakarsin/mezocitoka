- South African queen of Kwaito Mshoza has sadly passed away and fans are shaken to the core

- The media personality allegedly passed away in a hospital in Johannesburg on Thursday morning

- South African social media users expressed their shock and dismay over her untimely death

Kwaito lovers have been left reeling at the news of local singer Mshoza's sudden and unexpected death.

The media personality, whose real name is Nomasonto Maswangayi, allegedly passed on in a hospital in Joburg on Thursday, November 19, morning.

The details of her death had not yet been released at the time of publishing this piece.

Her passing on was first announced by industry peers such as Khanyi Mbau and Phil Phela.

It was later confirmed by the Daily Sun. The 37-year old hitmaker of Kortez leaves behind a colourful legacy in the South African music industry.

Social media users still cannot wrap their head around the news of the musician's death and all they could do was pray and hope she was in a better place.

Her fans were plunged into sadness and they recalled the impact her music had on their lives.

Friends and colleagues described Mshozi as a ray of sunshine whose light was taken too early.

Mshoza began singing at the tender age of 10.

She performed as a kwaito dancer between 1998 and 1999.

At 15 years old, the starlet was signed to a label.

Mshoza had been married three times in her life. She was first married to businessman Jacob Mnisi in 2007 and had two children.

The marriage ended in 2011 after reports of both being unfaithful.
