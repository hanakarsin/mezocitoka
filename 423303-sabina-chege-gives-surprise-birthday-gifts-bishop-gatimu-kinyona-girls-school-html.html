Sabina Chege Gives Her Surprise Birthday Gifts to Bishop Gatimu Kinyona Girls School

 Posted on May 6, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  404 words  |  Enda Jantzen
  • Sabina Chege's friends and family recently surprised her with a birthday party as she turned 49
  • In a philanthropic move, she announced that all the gifts she received will be donated to Bishop Gatimu Kinyona Girls School
  • On top of that, she has initiated a course to build a dormitory worth KSh 15 million for the needy school

Murang'a women representative Sabina Chege just turned 49, a milestone that was marked by a surprise birthday party from her family, colleagues, and friends.

The elated mother of three shared the exciting news on Facebook while thanking the kindhearted folks who successfully planned the event.

She appreciated the fact that it must have been a ton of work to not only bring everything together but also gather the participants while ensuring the plan remained a secret.

"My heart skipped a few beats when everyone shouted “surprise!” I think I could literally feel it skip," she said.

In attendance were other women leaders Anne Waiguru and Naisula Lesuuda among others, as well as Mugithi singers Samidoh and Jose Gatutura.

Donated Birthday Gifts

To make the birthday count, Sabina announced that all the birthday gifts she received will be donated to Bishop Gatimu Kinyona Girls, who urgently need a dormitory.

"It will cost me around KSh 15 million to have a fully operational one," she wrote.

She invited other philanthropists to join the good course by sending her a message for more details.

From Grass to Grace

Sabina rose from being a lowly-paid actress at the Kenya National Theatre to a radio host before crossing into politics.

She also acted as Rehema on Kenya Broadcasting Corporation's television soap Tausi in the mid-90s.

Throughout her radio career, her prime points of focus were development and women issues, a passion she has carried into her current dispensation.
