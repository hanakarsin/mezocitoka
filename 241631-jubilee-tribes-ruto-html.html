Jubilee is for just two tribes - Ruto

 Posted on March 14, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  380 words  |  Chauncey Koziol

- President Uhuru and his deputy have been labeled as tribal leaders

- A NASA principal has claimed the two are leading on two tribes in Kenya when there are more than 40 others

- NASA and Jubilee will face off in the August 8 polls

- The Jubilee administration has been accused of the high cost of living in the country

President Uhuru Kenyatta and his deputy William Ruto's Jubilee administration has been labelled a concoction of just two tribes when there are more than 40 in Kenya.

The attack was meted on the two on Thursday, May 25, by Bomet Governor Isaac Ruto who was speaking in a rally by his Chama Cha Mashinani party in Narok county.

The fiery former Jubilee governor said that Kenya has come a long way to be led by two tribal leaders who care less about the rest of the Kenyans.

"We want a non-tribal Kenya for all the 43 tribes, not the one dominated by two tribes as exhibited by Jubilee," Ruto said as quoted by Standard.

He also hit out at the administration for the high cost of living, saying it is only NASA that can provide soultions to Kenyans.

Ruto was elected on a Jubilee ticket as Bomet Governor and was a close ally and defender of DP Ruto before they had a major fallout over the dissolution of the United Republican Party (URP) to form the Jubilee party.

His fallout also came when he was chairman of the council of governors where he accused the national government of withholding funds meant for the devolved units.

Ruto has since formed his own party and ditched Jubilee to become the fifth principal in the NASA coalition.

Others in the coalition are flag bearer Raila Odinga, his deputy Kalonzo Musyoka, Chief Minister Musalia Mudavadi and his other assistant Moses Wetangula.
