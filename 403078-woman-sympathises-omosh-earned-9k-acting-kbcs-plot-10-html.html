Woman sympathises with Omosh, says she earned 9k acting KBC's Plot 10

 Posted on July 31, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  538 words  |  Chauncey Koziol

- A woman shared her predicament on Twitter on how she earned low wages while acting KBC's Plot 10

- She said she would get paid KSh 3,000 per episode and she had to quit after four months

- Kenyans flocked her comment section admitting that appearing on TV does not equal to a fat pay check

The heartbreaking story of how former Tahidi High actor Peter Kinuthia known by his stage name as Omosh was languishing in poverty brought many to tears.

The celebrated actor broke down in tears explaining how life had dealt him a hard blow as he was facing eviction and was struggling to keep his family afloat.

A Kenyan lady also took to Twitter to sympathise with the thespian and also recalled the meagre wages she earned while acting KBC's Plot 10.

In a bid to bring to light the struggles that actors face in the industry, the woman, identified as Chepkorir said though the show was a hit, she only earned KSh 3,000 per episode.

Her monthly take-home was therefore KSh 9,000 because the show only aired a few episodes per month.

"Omosh took me back to 2012. I was an actress playing 'Wendy' on Plot 10 KBC. Salary was 30$ (3k) per episode. Air in advance, take home is 9K. I quit after 4 months," she said.

Lucky for her that by that time, she was still in school so her parents would take care of her expenses, but nonetheless, she had to quit because the wages were just too little.

Kenyans flooded the comment section of her post admitting that sometimes being a celebrity is not as glamorous as it looks.

RodgerThat@rodgersowaga said:

"Appearing on TV does not make one a celebrity. Takes a lot more to be celebrated."

Joseph Mangeli said:

"I am surprised there is no money in the industry. I thought you guys have already made it in lie."

Munyalo Musyoki said:

"Our society has a stereotype of anyone that appears on TV on a regular got chums."

Fortunately for Omosh, Kenyans from all walks of life have come to his rescue.

As reported by TUKO.co.ke, Zero Hero Properties Limited, a real estate company, decided to give him a Valentines' Day with a difference.

Staff from the company not only spent the day with him but also extended a parcel of land to the TV star, complete with a title deed.

Comedian Jalang'o also promised to raise KSh 1 million for the actor to rebuild his life.
