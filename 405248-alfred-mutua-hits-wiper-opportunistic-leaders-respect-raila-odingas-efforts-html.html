Maendeleo Chap Chap party leader and Machakos governor Alfred Mutua has praised ODM boss Raila Odinga for the historic March 18 handshake.

In a statement on Tuesday, March 9, Mutua laughed off at some leaders whom he said benefited from the handshake only for them to demand Raila to shelve his ambitions in their favour.

"Some highly opportunistic leaders who were not part of the handshake and who have benefited from the handshake are asking Raila to forego the handshake benefits in their favour. Talk of hyena waiting for the spoils after the hunt.....Leaders need to stop being opportunistic," he stated.

Mutua was reacting to Wiper MPs who told off Raila's camp over their apparent insecurities arising from Raila's association with President Uhuru Kenyatta.

Led by Kitui senator Enock Wambua, the politicians castigated their ODM for demanding too much from Uhuru asking Raila to walk out of the political marriage if he was not satisfied.

"These people have been invited in President Uhuru Kenyatta's government but they now want to dictate who works where and does what in the office of the president, it can't happen. If ODM is not satisfied with the terms and conditions of the handshake, let them quit, we can do that job," said Wambua.

On his part, Mavoko MP Patrick Makau who hosted the event said Wiper party leader Kalonzo Musyoka was ready to work with the president to the end.

He asked Raila to walk out of the handshake arrangement if he was feeling shortchanged with the president.

"I'm very glad that the president has been working closely with Kalonzo and has faith in him. We will continue working with his government to the end," said Makau.

Undermining handshake

Senator James Orengo recently kicked off the storm when he claimed certain powerful individuals within the government were plotting to hijack the Building Bridges Initiative.

The constitutional amendment initiative was a product of the Uhuru-Raila agreement.

"As Uhuru and Raila are talking about BBI, there is a cabal of civil servants inside the government who have already decided who will rule Kenya in 2022. We saw it during Kibaki's time and Mzee Jomo Kenyatta's time. This is a warning shot to these people, they are trying to derail us, and we will expose them" Orengo said on Saturday, March 6.
