Govt' Targets to Train 220k Jobless Teachers on CBC in One Year Diploma Course

 Posted on February 20, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  449 words  |  Lourie Helzer
  • The last batch of P1 teachers graduated in December 2020 after a rigorous certificate course training
  • The government had hoped diploma students would join its 35 teachers training colleges in July 2021
  • However, only 400 applicants showed interest in diploma in primary teacher education

The government intends to train jobless teachers to prepare them for the extensive Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC).

Basic Education Principal Secretary Julius Jwan said the ministry is targeting at least 220,000 who are trained but jobless teachers majority of whom are P1 teachers who graduated in 2020.

The programme will see this batch of teachers spread across 30 teacher training colleges for a one-year diploma course to equip them with the necessary skills.

The P1 teachers passed out in December 2020, Jwan disclosed the first diploma in primary teacher education (DPTE) group joined in July 2021.

The diploma programme only attracted 400 whom the government distributed to Thogoto, Machakos, Igoji, Baringo and Migori teacher training colleges.

This means 30 other colleges did not receive any DPTE students.

The trend was also witnessed in the Early Childhood and Development Education (ECDE) sector where the ministry only received 200 qualified applicants.

Scrapping P1 teachers

Going forward, the government will scrap out the P1 Teachers course and replacing it with the DPTE course to enhance the quality of basic education.

The minimum entry requirement was a C plain or its equivalent in the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) as well as a C plain in English, Kiswahili, Mathematics, one in a Humanities subject, and one science subject.

Further details reveal that out of the 600 qualified students from both primary and ECDE, 100 did not join the institutions, leaving only 50 students.

Teachers feted

In another development, the Teachers Service Commission honoured 31 tutors for exemplary work.

The TSC chief executive officer Nancy Macharia said the 31 teachers demonstrated exemplary performance in teaching, management and effective delivery of the curriculum for quality education.

Macharia said the teachers were picked based on TSC's performance recognition, reward and sanction policy framework.

"I wish to state that we are not in any way suggesting that the 31 teachers are the only remarkable teachers we have in the practice. In fact, this number is just the tip of the iceberg. "We, at the TSC, will keep finding more ways to ensure all our teachers feel recognised and appreciated for their work," Macharia said.
