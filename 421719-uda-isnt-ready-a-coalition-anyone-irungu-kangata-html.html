Murang'a Senator Irungu Kang'ata has said that United Democratic Alliance (UDA), led by Deputy President William Ruto, is not ready to form a coalition with anyone.

UDA to be national party

The senator said allies of Ruto were pushing for a party that has a national outlook which, according to him, is UDA.

"We and our supporters we are determined to push for a party that has national look which is UDA. We cannot talk of formation a coalition at this moment," Irungu said.

Regional party ahead of 2022 elections

Kang'ata said he was aware that some political leaders from Mt. Kenya were rooting for the formation of the regional party ahead of the 2022 General Election.

"Those planning to establish a regional party want to draw Kenyans back to a period that has only brought weeping, wailing, and gnashing of teeth, but us...we belong to a ununfying party called UDA," he said.

Kang'ata, a former Senate majority whip, stated they (UDA) can not stop any party from being formed since Kenya was a multiparty democracy.

"We as UDA members respect those who do not want want to join UDA, but for us and our supporters we shall not be joining the regional party," he added.

The lawmaker further stated that forming a party purportedly to negotiate with the Ruto was irrational and does not make sense.

"For me, a coalition should be post-elections but that does not mean we are amenable to any form of discussion," the lawmaker said.

Moses Kuria absent in Karen UDA meet

In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke reported that a section of Kenyans questioned the absence of Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria in Karen during a UDA meeting.

Known to be Ruto's staunch ally, Kuria, who currently heads the Chama Cha Kazi (CCK) party, could not be spotted in the DP's presser, leaving many to question his whereabouts.

According to Garissa Township MP Aden Duale, the meeting was only for UDA members.

However, he recognised George Koimburi, who won the Juja by-election through the People Empowerment Party (PEP), formerly led by Kuria.
