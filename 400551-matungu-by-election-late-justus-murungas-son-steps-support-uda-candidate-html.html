Matungu by-election: Late Justus Murunga's son steps down to support UDA candidate

 Posted on May 24, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  512 words  |  Hettie Henneman

- Eugene Musungu had announced he would vie for the Matungu seat that was left vacant by his father on an independent ticket

- He has, however, stepped down in favour of UDA's Alex Lanya who has expressed confidence in winning the by-election

- Murunga's widow Christabel Murunga had also expressed interest in succeeding her husband but she was not cleared by IEBC

The son of the late Matungu MP who had expressed interest in succeeding his father Justus Murunga has stepped down in favour of United Democratic Alliance (UDA) party's candidate, Alex Lanya.

Eugene Murunga had opted to vie as an independent candidate after it proved difficult to get the backing of major political parties.

His stepping down was announced on the official UDA Twitter handle on Thursday, January 21, noting the party had welcomed his decision to support Lanya.

"We welcome Eugene Murunga selfless act of stepping down in favour of our candidate Alex Lanya. Eugene is the son of the late Hon. Murunga," UDA tweeted.

Murunga’s widow, Christabel Murunga and son Eugene had joined the race to succeed him, in the Matungu by-election.

The two were to vie as independent candidates and they were among the 23 aspirants who have submitted their documents for vetting by the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) ahead of the March 4, 2021, by-election.

The first to be cleared was the UDA candidate Alex Lanya, who was accompanied by Deputy President William Ruto’s allies including former Sports Cabinet Secretary Rashid Echesa, Mumias East MP Benjamin Washiali and his Sirisia counterpart John Waluke.

Lanya said he was confident he would win the seat.

“I am happy that I have sailed through the first hurdle and now we will be going to launch our campaign and sell our agenda to the people of Matungu,” said Lanya.

Amani National Congress (ANC) candidate Peter Nabulindo also got the IEBC nod to vie in the by-election.

Nabulindo was accompanied by ANC leader Musalia Mudavadi, Ford Kenya leader Moses Wetang’ula and a host of MPs. United Democratic Party candidate Charles Kasamani was also cleared.

However, the Murunga’s widow Christabel Amunga was not cleared as she lacked requisite documents.

The Matungu parliamentary seat fell vacant following the death of MP Justus Murunga on November 14, 2020.

The Amani National Congress (ANC) elected MP died while he was being rushed to the hospital, with multiple reports indicating that he developed breathing complications.

He was also battling diabetes.
