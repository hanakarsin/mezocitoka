10 best philosophy podcasts you should check out in 2021

Gone are the days when people relied on traditional media for entertainment, information, and education. Today, technology has enabled more people to own spaces where they can communicate their thoughts and form a huge following. Podcasts are increasing in number as with the right equipment, you can share what you have with anyone in any part of the world. Philosophy is one of the most intriguing topics people talk about. The best philosophy podcasts have different philosophers share their wisdom and experiences with listeners who are eager to learn from them.

Spotify is one of the most reliable mediums for podcasts. Philosophy podcasts on Spotify are easy to follow as their layout is straightforward. To enjoy the podcasts, you have to subscribe and sometimes follow the podcasters on their social media platforms so that you don't miss out when they share their content.

10 best philosophy podcasts

Philosophy podcasts are an invaluable way to increase your health, wealth, and wisdom. The study of knowledge and existence can only broaden your horizons and improve your quality of life. Here are the top 10 podcasts you should listen to:

1. 5 Questions

5 Questions is a podcast by Kieran Setiya. The podcaster interviews and has conversations with philosophers and scholars. You can catch new episodes on Tuesday. 5 Questions is available on Spotify, Apple Podcasts, Breaker, Castbox, and Google Podcasts, among other media.

2. The 80,000 Hours Podcast

The 80,000 Hours Podcast is hosted by Rob Wiblin, a genetics and economics graduate. He is also assisted by Arden Kohler and Ben Todd. The podcast is about issues that affect the world population, with the host inviting experts that can help people solve their problems through their careers. This podcast is available on Apple Podcasts, Google Podcasts, Spotify, and Podcast Addict.

3. After Dinner Conversation

Do you love short stories about human experiences? Kolby Granville got you. After Dinner Conversations is a podcast about basically everything that happens in life.

Think of people's believes, education, dealing with personal struggles, and achieving your goals. Kolby Granville discusses everything in the podcast while sharing fascinating tales.

4. Always Ready Podcast

In Always Ready Podcast, you will listen to discussions and interviews with B Aultman, Rachel Brown, Emily Crandall, John McMahon, and James Padilioni, Jr. as they host authors, activists, political figures, and leaders. Every episode of this podcast is fresher than the previous.

The panel talks about politics, economics, and other pressing topics. Always Ready Podcast is among the best political philosophy podcasts on Spotify.

5. Annares Project for Alternative Futures

Joseph Orosco holds the most intriguing discussions where he engages his listeners on insightful topics in Annares Project for Alternative Futures. His discussions are sober, and the host brings over guests from different backgrounds. Catch Joseph and the unconventional topics he discusses on his Podcast on Soundcloud.

6. Armchair Opinions

This podcast is hosted by Alex Impey, Carl Messenger, and James Brown. The hosts and guests are fun to listen to and often answer questions asked by listeners. They cover topics like being childfree, the existence of aliens, and self-improvement. Armchair Opinions is one of the best philosophy podcasts for beginners.

7. Bad Philosophy

Stephen Torrence and Kevin Saunders will engage you in what they call bad philosophy in Bad Philosophy. The topics they discuss are random but exciting. What makes this podcast exciting to listen to is how the hosts deliver what they have. You can feel the authenticity in their content even when they disagree on matters.

8. Black Mirror Reflections

Do you fancy conversations that revolve around technology, philosophy, morality, and politics? Then Black Mirror Reflections is the podcast for you. The only downside to this podcast is that you need to have at least watched a couple of episodes of the show to get some references. The host, Leigh M. Johnson, invites different guests to discuss each episode.

9. Brain in a Vat

Are you looking for philosophy podcasts on YouTube? Start with Brain in a Vat. The podcast is hosted by Dr Jason Werbeloff and Mark Oppenheimer. The two interview philosophers, picking their brains on various topics that involve societal norms, innovations, and generally how people live.

10. Can You Phil It?

Jeanne Proust and Johnny Nicholson describe Can You Phil It? as an interdisciplinary podcast that uses a philosophical lens to explore any topic. Some of the key things discussed include death, time, consciousness, and happiness. In addition, the hosts break down philosophical jargon and answer some of the most difficult questions human beings often ask themselves.
