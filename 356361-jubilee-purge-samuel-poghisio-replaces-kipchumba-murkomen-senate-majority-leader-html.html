Jubilee purge: Samuel Poghisio replaces Kipchumba Murkomen as Senate Majority leader

 Posted on March 7, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  338 words  |  Lourie Helzer

- Senator Susan Kihika was replaced by Irungu Kang'ata as the Majority Whip to be deputised by Farhiya Ali

- Kipchumba Murkomen was ousted from his Senate Majority seat and was replaced by Senator Samuel Poghisio

- The changes were made during a Jubilee PG meeting which DP Ruto's allies reportedly snubbed

It is now official, Senator Kipchumba Murkomen has been ousted from the Senate Majority seat and West Pokot Senator Samuel Poghisio has consequently replaced him.

Isiolo Senator Fatuma Dullo was elected as deputy majority leader while Senator Irungu Kanga'ta replaced Senator Susan Kihika as the new Majority Whip to be deputised by Farhiya Ali.

The House changes were made during a Jubilee Party Parliamentary Group Meeting which was convened on Monday, May 11, at State House, a few days after the ruling party and KANU entered into a post-poll coalition agreement.

The ouster of key Deputy President William Ruto's allies is set to deal a major blow to his camp with President Uhuru Kenyatta's power and control in the House given a major boost by the changes.

Senators allied to the DP were reportedly under instruction to snub the PG meeting over what they called the personalisation of party matters by the president.

"Jubilee is a national party and is funded by the taxpayers, we owe it to the taxpayers in running affairs of the party, we cannot personalise party issues," said the party's deputy secretary general Caleb Kositany.

The Jubilee-Kanu coalition agreement will give the indipendent party's legislators power to attend the ruling party's PG meetings.
