A look into the life of renowned author Margret Ogola as Google celebrates her

 Posted on March 24, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  399 words  |  Almeda Bohannan

Today, Wednesday, June 12, would have seen late celebrated author Margaret Ogola turn 60 were she alive.

The author was born in June 12, 1958 and died in September 21, 2011.

For close to a decade and a half, Ogola, a pediatrician and health administrator, juggled her stethoscope and pen quite successfully.

Away from writing, she was the Medical Director of Cottolengo Hospice for HIV and Aids orphans from 1994.

She received the Familias Award for Humanitarian Service of the World Congress of Families in 1999 in Geneva, Switzerland.

The author burst into the Kenyan literary scene in the mid-1990s with her award winning debut novel The River and the Source.

This was shortly after dropping a manuscript for her book titled Mandate of the People, at Focus Publishers.

The new novel put her title tally to six; four novels, a biography and a parenting guide.

With her hands full of professional as well as family and community service, Ogola said she wrote mostly in the night while confessing she was a poor sleeper.

Her book, The River and the Source, lifted the Commonwealth Writers Prize Africa region and Jomo Kenyatta Prize for Literature in 1995.

Despite battling cancer, Ogola managed to pen a third novel, Place of Destiny, which won her a second Jomo Kenyatta Prize for Literature in 2007.

It is a semi-autobiographical tale of a woman dying of cancer that could as well turn out to have been a premonition treatise.

Her other books are I Swear by Apollo, a sequel to The River and the Source and Cardinal Otunga: A Gift of Grace, a biography which she co-authored with Margaret Roche.

She also wrote Educating in Human Love, a handbook on sex education she wrote with her husband, George Ogola.

The iconic woman was on Wednesday, June 12, celebrated by Google with a unique doodle to mark her 60th birthday
