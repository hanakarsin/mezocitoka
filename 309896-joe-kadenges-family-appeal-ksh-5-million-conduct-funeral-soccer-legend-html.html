Joe Kadenge's family appeals for KSh 5 million to conduct soccer legend's funeral

 Posted on March 8, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  369 words  |  Almeda Bohannan

- 84-year-old Joe Kadenge died on Sunday, July 7 after long ailment

- Funeral and burial arrangement will cost KSh 5 million according to his family

- A requiem mass is slated for Wednesday, July 17, at Friends Internal Centre three days ahead of internment

Funeral and burial arrangement for the late soccer idol Joe Kadenge of Kadenge na Mpira fame will roughly cost KSh 5 million.

However, the family is facing financial challenges and is currently unable to raise particular thus appealed for support to help give their kin a befitting send off.

"We have sat down as a family. We have also estimated the funeral expenses and it comes to KSh 5 million from mortuary to transport of the body to rural home," family spokesman Chris Mbwamba said on Tuesday, July 9.

The body of Kadenge who passed on Sunday, July 7, will be laid to rest on Saturday, July 17 in Kakamega county.

A requiem mass is slated for Wednesday, July 17, at Friends Internal Centre.

The 84-year-old Kenyan great, had a decline in his health since he suffered a stroke in February 2019.

Kadenge's stroke was the second one he suffered in 10 years, and has been in and out of hospital since February as doctors closely monitored his situation.

Kadenge's sunset years have not been the most beautiful as he was wasting away from the limelight before he went viral with an urgent plea to see President Uhuru Kenyatta in 2017.

"I would like to meet you before I die. It seems my body doesn't like me anymore and these are the people I would like to meet. The old men, I would like to say goodbye to them so that if God calls me today, I will have an opportunity to say good bye to them,"he pleaded then.
