Poll shows 65% of Kenyans support calls for referendum

 Posted on May 25, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  382 words  |  Almeda Bohannan

Majority of Kenyans support the calls for a referendum to amend the Constitution, a recent survey by Infotrak shows.

The survey conducted between Saturday, February 9, and Monday, February 11, covering eight regions; Central, Rift Valley, Nyanza, Coast, Nairobi, North Eastern, Western and Eastern showed 65% of the adult population supported calls for the plebiscite.

According to the findings released on March, February 25, the number of Kenyans supporting the referendum significantly increased from 44% in the last quarter of 2018 to the current 65% in the first quarter of 2019.

Infotrak in the survey which covered 800 participants, concluded that the support for constitutional change was significantly lower in Central and Eastern regions compared to the other six regions.

The eight regions represent the defunct eight provinces which were rendered obsolete with the promulgation of the current Constitution in 2010.

"After eight years of implementation of the Constitution, several gaps and ambiguities in the supreme document have been pointed out. A majority (52%) of Kenyans thus expressed their support for a referendum to fill gaps and address the ambiguous provisions of the Constitution," read the survey findings.

The study indicated 38% of Kenyans, however, supported the referendum to reduce number of elected leaders and subsequently the public wage bill burdening taxpayers.

"Perhaps they have a feeling that there is over-representation in both Parliament and the County Assemblies. 10% of Kenyans especially from Opposition strongholds support the referendum in order to expand the Executive and create more positions to ensure inclusivity," concluded the survey.

Support for the proposed referendum was highest in Nyanza, North Eastern and Coast regions at 78%, 72% and 70% respectively.

The support for the plebiscite was, however, lowest in Central and Eastern regions at 55% and 59% respectively.
